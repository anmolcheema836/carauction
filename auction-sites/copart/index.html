Copart UK Fees & Buying Guide

Copart is the dominant player in the UK salvage market. They sell vehicles on behalf of insurance companies, fleet operators, and finance companies. While you can find bargains, the fee structure and rules differ significantly from traditional auctions.

Who is it for?

Copart sells to both trade members and the general public (for certain vehicles). However, many "Category B" breaker vehicles are restricted to licensed dismantlers only.

⚠️ The 10% Relist Warning

If you win a vehicle on Copart and fail to pay, or change your mind, you will be charged 10% of your bid price (minimum £250) as a relist fee. Do not bid unless you have the funds ready and are certain about the car.

Cost Categories

Unlike BCA, Copart fees are heavily weighted towards "Internet Bid Fees" and "Gate Fees".

  • Member Fees: Tiered based on sale price.
  • Internet Bid Fee: An extra charge for bidding online (which is how almost everyone bids).
  • Gate Fee: (~£50) A charge for the administrative process of moving the car out of the yard.
  • Loading Fee: If the car is non-runners/salvage, you will be charged for a forklift load.

Salvage Warnings

Most vehicles are sold as "Run and Drive" or "Non-Runner". However, even "Run and Drive" only guarantees the vehicle moves under its own power forward and back. It does not guarantee roadworthiness. Always perform a vehicle history check (HPI) to see the write-off category (Cat S, Cat N).

Check The Fees First

Don't get caught out by gate fees and loading charges.
