BCA Buyer Fees Explained (UK)

BCA (British Car Auctions) is the UK's largest vehicle remarketing company. While they offer huge variety, their fee structure can be complex for new buyers. This guide explains the costs involved.

How Buying Works

Most BCA auctions are now digital-first (BCA Online or Bid Now). The process generally involves:

  1. Registering: You typically need a trade account or a "MyBCA" account.
  2. Browsing: View catalogues online. Look for "BCA Assured" reports.
  3. Bidding: Live online or via proxy bid.
  4. Paying & Collecting: Strict deadlines apply (usually 24-48 hours).

Buyer Fees: How They Are Structured

BCA fees are not fixed percentages; they are tiered based on the "Hammer Price". Furthermore, the fee you pay depends on your card status (Standard, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Black).

Important: Most one-time or small-volume buyers fall into the "Standard" rate, which is the most expensive tier.

Other Costs to Budget For

  • BCA Assured Fee: (~£45-£60) A mandatory mechanical check fee on eligible cars.
  • V5 / Admin Fee: (~£25) For handling the logbook transfer.
  • Online Fee: (~£40+) An additional fee for using the digital bidding platform.
  • VAT: 20% VAT is added to all of the fees mentioned above.

Common Mistakes

The biggest mistake new buyers make is assuming a £5,000 bid means they pay £5,000. In reality, with a Standard account, a £5,000 hammer price could result in a total invoice closer to £5,800 or £6,000 once all fees and VAT are added.
